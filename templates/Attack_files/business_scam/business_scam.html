Advisory Summary

The U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) has determined that {{ name }} is not registered with the CFTC and holds no license to solicit or accept funds from U.S. residents. Operating without the required licenses is illegal. This entity is not allowed to operate in the United States and has been placed on a public warning list for unlawfully targeting U.S. persons.

Identified Risks & Red Flags

  • Not Registered or Licensed

    This entity holds no valid license and is not registered with the CFTC as a Futures Commission Merchant (FCM). It is therefore not allowed to solicit U.S. customers or handle their funds.

  • Misleading Claims of High Profitability

    The entity makes unrealistic promises of high, guaranteed returns from trading forex, binary options, or cryptocurrencies, which is a hallmark of investment fraud.

  • Issues with Fund Withdrawals

    A significant number of complaints allege that the entity makes it difficult or impossible for customers to withdraw their funds, often demanding additional "taxes" or "fees".

  • Use of Unregulated Payment Channels

    The entity often requires deposits via irreversible methods like cryptocurrency transfers or wire transfers, which limits a customer's ability to dispute transactions.

Common Deceptive Tactics

Unregistered entities like {{ name }} frequently employ specific tactics to appear legitimate. Be aware of:

  • Professional-Looking Websites & Apps

    Scammers create sophisticated platforms that mimic real trading sites, complete with fake charts and account balances to deceive investors.

  • Social Media & Influencer Marketing

    They often use social media platforms and pay influencers to promote their services, creating a false sense of popularity and trust.

Actions for Consumers

The CFTC strongly warns all consumers to avoid dealing with unlicensed and unregistered entities like {{ name }}. If you have already been in contact, take the following steps immediately:

  1. Cease All Transactions: Do not send any more money to this entity.
  2. Preserve All Records: Compile all records of communication and transactions.
  3. File a Complaint: Submit a tip or complaint to the CFTC's Division of Enforcement.
